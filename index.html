The Mountains

Mountains are prominent landforms that have significant heights above sea level and/or the surrounding land. They are steeper than hills. A mountain or mountain range usually has a peak, which is a pointed top. Mountains have different climates than land at sea level and nearby flat land.

Most geologists classify a mountain as a landform that rises at least 1,000 feet (300 meters) or more above its surrounding area. A mountain range is a series or chain of mountains that are close together.

A mountain is landform that rises prominently above its surroundings. It usually has steep slopes, and a summit area. The highest point of a mountain is called the peak. A mountain's summit is the highest area where an individual can reach.
